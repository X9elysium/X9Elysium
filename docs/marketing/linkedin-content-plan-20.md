## Theme A — Green (matte black + emerald) — pros / cons

**Pros**
- 1:1 match with x9elysium.com — every click from LinkedIn lands on a brand the prospect already recognizes.
- Stands out in a feed dominated by white/corporate templates. Pattern-interrupt = scroll-stop.
- Premium + founder-led signal. Feels like Linear/Arc/Stripe, not Salesforce.
- Compounds across surfaces — IG plan, /chat UI, site hero, decks all read as one entity.
- Carries Naval-cadence copy well — short clauses on dark backgrounds feel earned.

**Cons**
- Heavy contrast at scale = brand fatigue if every post looks identical. Solve with layout variance, not color drift.
- Native LinkedIn polls render in a white card — green carousel + white poll feels stitched. Mitigate: pair the poll with a green companion image (or use polls sparingly).
- Screenshots (dashboards, code, Plus admin) photograph badly on dark — readability drops. Solve with framed screenshots in dark cards, never raw.
- Some prospects — ops/finance buyers — still associate dark mode with "tech" not "consulting." Solve via copy posture (terse, founder-led), not aesthetic compromise.
- Accessibility: low-contrast secondary text fails WCAG AA. Lock body copy at white-on-black `#FFFFFF`/`#0a0a0a` and emerald only for accent words.

## Theme B — White (clean / blue) — pros / cons

**Pros**
- Highest legibility, especially on mobile in bright light.
- Plays nicely with native LinkedIn UI — feels like an institutional message rather than a brand flex.
- Better canvas for screenshot-heavy posts: dashboards, GraphQL outputs, admin UI, Tableau snapshots.
- Reads more "trustworthy" to compliance-minded buyers (legal, finance, enterprise procurement).
- Easier to repurpose into client decks and proposals without restyling.

**Cons**
- Off-brand from x9elysium.com — breaks the visual moat. A prospect who saw your last post in green and clicks through to a green site, then sees a white post, registers inconsistency.
- Blanderizes the feed presence. Looks like every other agency. The whole point of the matte-black is it doesn't look like an agency.
- Loses the "founder-led, no juniors" signal that the green carries silently.
- White + blue is closer to a Salesforce/HubSpot template — it broadcasts the wrong stack association (we're not SaaS, we're consulting).

**Use white only when:**
- Announcing a real, verifiable milestone (case study live, partner status approved, hire confirmed, Supreme launched).
- Posting a content piece where institutional clarity beats brand voice (compliance/regulatory, formal partnership, hiring announcement).
- Sharing a screenshot-heavy comparison where dark mode degrades readability.

## Audience targeting

### LinkedIn — primary audience

**Tier 1 (warmest — convert):**
- Founders / CEOs of DTC + B2B brands on Shopify Plus, $5M–$50M GMV
- COO / VP Ops at the same band
- Heads of Ecommerce / Digital at sub-$100M brands evaluating Plus migration

**Tier 2 (compounding — trust):**
- Ecom CTOs and senior engineers (technical credibility audience)
- Fractional CMO / fractional COO operators who refer brands
- Partner agencies — paid (Pilothouse, Common Thread), retention (Klaviyo partners), CRO (Mutiny) — for co-referral, not competition

**Tier 3 (signal — canvas):**
- Builders, indie founders, AI-curious operators — they amplify Naval-style takes, follow Darsh as a thinker, become future hires/partners

**Geography:** North America primary (Toronto/GTA, NYC, SF, LA, Austin), UK/EU secondary, AU/NZ tertiary.

**Sales Navigator / paid filters when promoting:**
- Job titles: Founder, Co-Founder, CEO, COO, VP Operations, Head of Ecommerce, Director of Ecommerce, CTO, VP Engineering
- Company headcount: 11–500
- Industry: Retail, Apparel & Fashion, Consumer Goods, Health/Wellness, Food & Beverage, Sporting Goods
- Seniority: Owner, Partner, CXO, VP, Director
- Tech filter (where available): Shopify Plus, Klaviyo, Recharge, Gorgias, Yotpo

### X.com — adaptation audience

Different audience, different tone. **Do not copy-paste LinkedIn → X.** Adapt:
- Builders, indie hackers, AI tinkerers, dev-curious founders, ecom Twitter (Andrew Faris, Drew Sanocki orbit)
- Aphoristic, shorter, more contrarian. Threads where LinkedIn gets carousels.

## X.com adaptation

For each green post, port to X as:
- **Single-image posts** → 1 tweet + image, lift the hook verbatim.
- **Carousels** → threads. One slide = one tweet.
- **Polls** → native X polls (4 options max — same structure).
- **Hot takes / aphorisms** → text-only tweets. These often outperform on X.
- **Video demo (#8)** → trim to 8–10s for X.
- **CTAs** → soften. X audience is allergic to "book a call" — frame as "we open 2 retainers per quarter, dm if curious."

Feed this into `data/x-thoughts.md` for the existing X automation (`scripts/x/`).
