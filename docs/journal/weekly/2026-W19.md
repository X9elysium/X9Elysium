## Learned this week

(See `lessons.md` for the long-form versions.)

- **Fabrications drift back in.** Stripped a quote on May 6, found it back on May 7. Structural defenses (replace the *shape*, not the words) beat editorial defenses.
- **Single-deploy CI > split build/deploy.** One job, one `out/`, one wrangler step. Halves wall time and removes a failure surface.
- **`paths-ignore` is mandatory for any cron-committing workflow.** Bot commits should not trigger rebuilds.
- **Synth-first / cloud-fallback is a truth posture in code.** Sanctuary's empty-state UX is the principle made concrete.
- **The under-claim posture forces the bar up.** The site reads sharper without the fabricated stats than it did with them.
