Google Shopping. Meta Advantage+ catalog ads. TikTok Shop. Pinterest Shopping. Microsoft Shopping. Amazon Sponsored Products. They all run on the same underlying asset: your product feed. For most brands the feed is an afterthought — Shopify auto-generates it, the agency installed Google Merchant Center years ago, nobody touches it. That neglect is leaking 20–40% of shopping-ad revenue. This post is the founder-level framework for treating the feed as the strategic asset it is.

## What's actually in a feed

A product feed for Google Shopping has roughly 70 supported attributes, of which about 12 are critical and 30 more are leverage-positive. The critical ones:

- `id`, `title`, `description`, `link`, `image_link`
- `price`, `availability`, `brand`
- `gtin` or `mpn`
- `condition`, `google_product_category`, `product_type`

The leverage attributes most stores skip:

- `additional_image_link` (multiple images per product, not just one)
- `gender`, `age_group`, `color`, `size`, `material`, `pattern` (apparel)
- `sale_price`, `sale_price_effective_date`
- `shipping_weight`, `custom_label_0` through `custom_label_4`

Each attribute the platform algorithm has access to is a signal it can use to match your product to the right shopper at the right moment. Sparse feeds get sparse impressions. Rich feeds get dramatically better placement.

## The Shopify-default problem

When you connect Shopify to Google Merchant Center via the Shopify Google channel app, you get a feed. It works. It's also missing or weak on:

- **Titles formatted for browsing, not search.** Shopify titles are written for your store. Search titles need to lead with brand + category + key attribute. "Acme Lightweight Trail Running Shoe — Men's, Black, Size 10" beats "Lightweight Trail Runner."
- **Descriptions identical across all platforms.** Each platform has different optimal lengths and emphases. One description does not fit all.
- **No custom labels.** Custom labels (`custom_label_0` through `4`) let you bid differently on bestsellers vs slow movers, new arrivals vs evergreen, high-margin vs low-margin. Without them, every product gets equal-priority bidding.
- **Single image per product.** Shopping ad CTR is 30–80% higher with multiple images vs single. Most Shopify-default feeds only ship the primary product image.
- **Generic categorization.** Google's product taxonomy has 5,000+ categories. Auto-mapping is approximate. Manual mapping to the most specific category lifts impressions and CTR.

## The optimization order of operations

**1. Titles.** Largest impact, most testable.

Title formulas for Shopping:

- *Apparel:* `[Brand] [Gender] [Product Type] [Material] [Color] [Size]`
- *Electronics:* `[Brand] [Model] [Key Spec] [Color] [Storage/Size]`
- *Home goods:* `[Brand] [Product Type] [Material] [Size/Capacity] [Color]`

Lead with brand if it's well-known. If not, lead with category. Include the most-searched attribute first. Test variants on top SKUs and roll the winner across the catalog.

**2. Images.** Almost as impactful as titles.

- White-background lifestyle for Shopping (Google standard).
- Lifestyle-context for Meta and Pinterest.
- Square aspect ratio (1:1) is the safest cross-platform default.
- Multiple images per product where supported (`additional_image_link`).
- Image quality: minimum 800×800, ideally 1600×1600.

The mistake most stores make: shipping the same image set to every platform. Different platforms reward different visual styles.

**3. GTINs and brand attribution.** Mechanical work, big payoff.

- Populate every GTIN you have access to.
- For private label products: register through GS1 ($50–$250/year/SKU range).
- Set `brand` accurately on every SKU. Don't leave it blank.

Google specifically deprioritizes products with missing GTIN/brand combinations. Closing this gap can lift Shopping impressions 25–60% on affected SKUs.

**4. Custom labels for bid strategy.**

Set `custom_label_0` through `custom_label_4` to enable smarter bidding. Common labels:

- `custom_label_0`: Margin tier (high/mid/low)
- `custom_label_1`: Inventory level (in-stock/low/very-low)
- `custom_label_2`: Performance tier (top-seller/standard/long-tail)
- `custom_label_3`: Seasonality (spring/summer/fall/winter/evergreen)
- `custom_label_4`: New / evergreen / clearance

Then in Google Ads, segment campaigns or ad groups by custom label to bid more aggressively on the products that actually return ROAS.

**5. Description optimization for AI surfaces.**

Google's AI-powered Shopping experiences (lens, AI overviews, conversational shopping) draw heavily from descriptions. The pattern that works:

- First sentence: clear product identity and primary use case.
- Second sentence: top 2–3 differentiators.
- Body: structured spec list (use bullets in description if the platform supports them).
- Last sentence: trust signal (warranty, returns, brand promise).

Don't just paste your PDP description into the feed. The optimal feed description is shorter, denser, and more attribute-rich than your PDP copy.

## The feed-management tool decision

For stores running material shopping spend, a dedicated feed-management tool is one of the highest-ROI investments you can make. The pattern:

- **Below $1M ARR / $5K monthly shopping spend.** Shopify Google channel + manual GMC tweaks. No dedicated tool yet.
- **$1M–$10M ARR / $5K–$50K monthly shopping spend.** DataFeedWatch ($60–$300/month) or Channable ($100–$400/month). Worth the cost.
- **$10M+ ARR / $50K+ monthly shopping spend.** Feedonomics or GoDataFeed. Enterprise feed orchestration. $1K–$5K/month range. Pays back through better channel-specific optimization and reduced disapprovals.

What the tools unlock:

- **Per-channel transformations.** Different titles for Google vs Meta vs TikTok from the same source data.
- **Dynamic rules.** "If margin > 40%, set custom_label_0 = 'high_margin'" kind of logic without hand-editing the feed.
- **Disapproval recovery.** Auto-fix common rejection reasons (missing fields, policy mismatches).
- **Multi-feed export.** One source of truth, many destination feeds.

## Meta Advantage+ catalog and TikTok Shop

The Meta and TikTok versions of this discipline matter just as much as Google:

- **Meta Advantage+ catalog ads** (formerly Dynamic Ads) use the same feed structure. Titles and images matter more than description because Advantage+ creative often crops aggressively.
- **TikTok Shop product feeds** reward video assets, lifestyle imagery, and short, punchy titles. Square images get cropped to vertical aspect on TikTok — design accordingly.
- **Pinterest Shopping** rewards aspirational lifestyle imagery and broad category hashtags in descriptions.

The brands winning across all four channels typically run a single source-of-truth feed (in Shopify or PIM) and use a feed manager to ship channel-specific transformations.

## What "feed health" looks like

Healthy feed metrics across platforms:

- **Google Merchant Center:** zero critical issues, fewer than 5% of products with warnings, 95%+ approval rate.
- **Meta Catalog Manager:** 95%+ products in Active status, no critical errors, regular feed refreshes.
- **TikTok Shop:** 90%+ product approval, video assets attached on top sellers.

Most stores we audit have 15–30% of their feed in some warning or disapproved state. Closing that gap is the single biggest unlock.
