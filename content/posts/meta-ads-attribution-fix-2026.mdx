Every founder who runs Meta ads at scale eventually hits the same wall: Meta says ROAS is X, your accounting says it's actually 0.6X to 1.5X depending on the day, and nobody on the team is sure which number to believe. This post is the reconciliation framework we use for $20K–$500K monthly Meta spend: where Meta's number is wrong, how to triangulate truth, and what the three-pillar measurement stack actually looks like.

## Why a single attribution number is impossible

There is no single "correct" way to attribute a sale to an ad. Every measurement system makes assumptions:

- **Last-click** ignores everything that happened before the final click.
- **Linear** spreads credit equally across touches, pretending all touches matter equally.
- **Time-decay** weights recent touches more, but the decay curve is arbitrary.
- **Data-driven** uses machine learning to allocate credit based on patterns, but you can't audit the model.
- **Platform-reported** (Meta, Google, TikTok all do this) credits the platform's own clicks generously and ignores cross-platform interaction.

The honest answer is that attribution is a *triangulation* problem. You use multiple imperfect signals and reconcile them. The brands that scale ad spend healthily are the ones running this triangulation as a standard discipline.

## The three pillars

**Pillar 1: Server-side platform reporting.**
Meta CAPI, Google Enhanced Conversions, TikTok Events API. This is your "what each platform thinks happened" signal. It's biased toward each platform crediting itself, but with strong first-party data the bias is bounded.

**Pillar 2: Independent multi-touch attribution.**
GA4 with proper UTM hygiene, or a dedicated tool like Triple Whale, Northbeam, Polar, or Rockerbox. Looks at the full journey, not just the last platform-touched event. Useful for cross-platform comparison.

**Pillar 3: Direct customer signal.**
Post-purchase surveys ("Where did you first hear about us?") and incrementality tests (geo holdouts, ghost campaigns). The only signal that doesn't depend on cookies, pixels, or platform self-reporting.

The reconciled view is: **Meta CAPI for daily campaign optimization. GA4/MTA for weekly channel-mix decisions. Surveys + incrementality for monthly strategic decisions.**

## Where Meta over-reports

The clearest case: a customer who already knew your brand searches your name on Google, clicks an organic result, and converts. They had also seen a Meta retargeting ad earlier in the week. Meta credits the conversion under the 7-day-click window. Brand search would have closed the sale anyway.

This pattern shows up everywhere on healthy DTC brands. The result: Meta-reported ROAS over-counts brand-driven conversions and under-counts how much Meta is actually creating *new* demand.

The mitigation is incrementality testing — see [our incrementality post](/blog/incrementality-testing-dtc-brands) — and segmenting your reporting between **prospecting** (cold audiences, where Meta's contribution is real) and **retargeting** (warm audiences, where Meta is mostly closing what other channels created).

## Where Meta under-reports

The flip side: iOS users who saw an ad on iPhone, didn't click immediately, opened the brand on desktop later that day, and converted. Meta has no way to connect those two events without first-party identifiers. The conversion shows up as "direct" or "organic" in Meta's view.

The mitigation is **better Event Match Quality**:

- Hash and ship as much customer data as possible: email, phone, first name, last name, IP, user agent, click ID.
- Use Shopify's native Meta CAPI integration as a baseline — it covers the heavy lift.
- Layer the Conversions API Gateway or a server-side GTM container for additional control.
- Maintain a retention policy on customer identifiers in compliance with Quebec Law 25 / GDPR / CCPA.

Healthy stores run EMQ scores of 7.5+ across purchase events. Most stores we audit are at 4–6. The fix is mechanical and lifts reported ROAS 15–35% with no ad-spend change.

## The reconciliation rhythm we run

For $50K+/month ad spend clients on retainer:

**Daily:** Meta-reported ROAS in Meta Ads Manager. Make tactical decisions inside Meta — kill underperforming creatives, scale winners. Trust Meta inside Meta.

**Weekly:** Reconciliation dashboard. Three columns: Meta-reported, MTA-attributed (GA4 or Northbeam), survey-reported. We expect them to diverge. The pattern of divergence is the signal.

**Monthly:** Channel-mix review. Where is the truth across the three signals? Are we over-investing in channels that look good in Meta but bad on surveys? Under-investing in channels that look bad in Meta but consistent on surveys?

**Quarterly:** Incrementality test. Pick the largest single channel by spend and test geo-holdout for 4 weeks. Recalibrate the channel-credit assumptions based on what the test reveals.

## The post-purchase survey: cheap, useful, underused

The single highest-ROI measurement tool most DTC brands ignore is the post-purchase survey. Add a single question to your thank-you page or first post-purchase email:

> "Where did you first hear about us?"
>
> Options: Instagram, TikTok, YouTube, Google, Friend, Podcast, In-store, Other

That's it. Force-choice, structured. Not "tell us your customer journey" — that's qualitative gold but useless for quantitative reconciliation.

What you'll find:

- **Friend / word-of-mouth is usually 15–30% of new customers** and is invisible to every paid platform.
- **Podcast and influencer attribution** that platforms credit to "direct" or generic categories shows up here.
- **Brand search vs paid search** distinction emerges over time as you cross-reference survey data with Google Ads vs organic.

Tools: Fairing (built for this), KnoCommerce, or even a basic Shopify post-purchase script. Budget $200–$1,000 CAD/month. Pays for itself the first time it reveals a misallocated budget line.

## What to do if your Meta is broken right now

If your Meta-reported ROAS isn't matching reality on your P&L, the order of operations:

1. **Audit EMQ scores.** Open Meta Events Manager, look at each event, look at Event Match Quality. Anything below 6 is fixable in 1–2 weeks.
2. **Verify Shopify's native Meta CAPI integration.** Often configured but not fully wired.
3. **Audit attribution windows.** Default 7-day-click 1-day-view is over-aggressive for prospecting. Try 7-day-click only on cold campaigns.
4. **Add post-purchase survey.** First-week data will reframe your thinking.
5. **Run a 4-week incrementality test on one channel.** Recalibrate the credit assumptions.

This sequence takes 6–10 weeks. The brand walks out with confidence in the numbers and usually 10–25% better ad efficiency from cleaner data.
