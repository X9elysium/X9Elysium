You did between $1M and $5M GMV last year on Shopify. You're not Skims and you're not your cousin's Etsy shop. You have one operator, maybe a part-time merchandiser, a freelance email lead, and a developer on retainer. The headcount is permanent. The to-do list is not.

This post is the daily-operator's playbook for putting Claude — specifically Opus 4.7 and Sonnet 4.6 — into the seams of a small Shopify ops team. We use [chillymoose.ca](https://chillymoose.ca) as the running example because they're the archetype: Canadian outdoor lifestyle brand, hundreds of SKUs across drinkware and coolers, a credible community story (Integrity Reforestation, Ontario Parks, Curling Canada), and the kind of catalog where a single person could either drown in admin or quietly automate half of it.

We're going to walk through a real week.

## What changed in 2026 (and why this post exists)

Three things happened in the first half of 2026 that turned Claude from "useful assistant" into "second operator at every desk":

1. **Opus 4.7 shipped in April with a 1M-token context window** at standard pricing. You can drop a year of customer reviews, your entire theme codebase, or twelve months of P&L into a single conversation and ask real questions. ([Anthropic / Opus 4.7](https://www.cloudzero.com/blog/claude-opus-4-7-pricing/))
2. **Sonnet 4.6** sits inside 1.2 points of Opus on coding benchmarks while running 17% faster and 40% cheaper. For 90% of daily store work it is the right default. ([BenchLM](https://benchlm.ai/compare/claude-opus-4-7-vs-claude-sonnet-4-6))
3. **The Shopify AI Toolkit dropped on April 9, 2026** — a free, open-source plugin that connects Claude Code (and Cursor, OpenAI Codex, Gemini CLI) directly to your Shopify store via 16 skill files covering products, inventory, orders, themes, and metafields. ([Shopify AI Toolkit](https://novadata.io/resources/news/shopify-ai-toolkit-claude-code-cursor-store-management-2026))

That third one is the operator unlock. Before April, Claude could write a product description. Now it can write the description, push it to the right product variant, regenerate the alt text, update the metafield, and commit the change to a draft theme — all from a natural-language brief.

## The Chilly Moose week — what Claude actually does

Let's run a week the way it actually plays out at a brand of this size. Every example below is something we'd run for a [chillymoose.ca](https://chillymoose.ca)-shaped store.

### Monday — Catalog and PDP work

The merchandiser opens her laptop. There are 18 new colorways of the **Brent Insulator** queued for a spring drop. Manufacturer specs are sitting in a Google Sheet.

**Old workflow:** copy specs, draft, write meta description, write alt text, build out variant differentiation, ship to Shopify. Nine to twelve minutes per SKU. Multiplied by 18 = the whole morning gone.

**With Claude:** she opens her saved Claude Project, "Chilly Moose PDP Author." The project is loaded with:

- The brand voice block — "confident, terse, Canadian; never use 'elevate,' 'curated,' or 'cutting-edge'; second person; sentence average under 14 words."
- Three example PDPs that ship the voice cleanly.
- A Product schema template with required fields.
- A note: never invent material claims, sourcing facts, or partnership names.

She drops the spec sheet in. Sonnet 4.6 produces:

- 18 PDPs with 80–110 word descriptions
- 18 SEO meta descriptions under 155 characters
- 18 alt-text strings per hero image
- A markdown table of variant differentiation lines (e.g., the matte-black Brent vs the spruce-green Brent)

She reads them in a single pass — twelve minutes. Fixes brand-voice slips on three. Pushes the lot to Shopify via the AI Toolkit's `products` skill in two commands. Done by 11:00 AM.

The same system runs for the **Algonquin Tumbler 580mL**, **25L Ice Box**, **35L Wheeled Explorer**. The only thing that changes is the brief.

> **The non-obvious tip:** the brand-voice prompt is more important than the model. We've seen $5M brands run perfectly serviceable PDP programs on Sonnet 4.6 with a sharp brief, and we've seen $30M brands waste Opus calls because their brief is "write a product description for our cooler."

### Tuesday — Customer support drafts

Inbox check: 47 tickets overnight. Most are tier-1.

- "Does the 25L Ice Box keep ice for 5 days at +25°C?"
- "Do you ship to Yellowknife and how long does it take?"
- "I bought the Algonquin tumbler last month and the lid is cracking."
- "Can the Wheeled Explorer go on a plane as checked baggage?"
- "Do you do bulk orders for curling clubs?"

The CX lead has a Sonnet 4.6 prompt with three things baked in:

1. The store's shipping, returns, and warranty policy as reference docs.
2. Five examples of replies in the brand voice — warm, direct, no apologetic over-explaining.
3. Tone rules — never promise what isn't policy; flag any reply that should be escalated.

Each ticket gets pasted in. Claude writes a draft reply in 3–8 seconds. The lead reads, edits 2–3 lines on average, and sends. Tickets that need a human decision (refunds outside policy, custom orders) are flagged by the model with `[ESCALATE]` so they go to her queue, not the auto-send queue.

**Time saved:** 40–60% on average handle time for tier-1. **Tickets that previously sat overnight:** answered before lunch.

You can wire this directly into Gorgias or Re:amaze with their Claude integrations, or you can keep it manual until volume justifies. At $1M–$3M GMV the manual version is fine.

### Wednesday — Email, ads, and content

The fractional email lead pings: Mother's Day flow needs three new variants. The paid social freelancer wants Meta ad copy for the spring cooler push. The founder wants a blog post on "how to keep ice cold for a 4-day Algonquin canoe trip."

**Email:** Sonnet 4.6 + Klaviyo. The email lead has a project loaded with the last 24 emails Chilly Moose has sent, segmented by which performed (open rate, CTR, attributed revenue). She asks Claude to write three Mother's Day variants that match the top-performing structures, with the Brent Insulator as the hero. Three variants in 90 seconds. She picks one, edits subject lines, ships.

**Ads:** the freelancer pulls the same project — but with the [Brent Insulator] PDP and three winning ad creatives from last fall pasted in. Sonnet 4.6 produces 8 primary text variants, 8 headlines, 4 descriptions. He pastes them into Meta Ads Manager. **Total time: 20 minutes for what used to be a half-day.**

**Long-form blog:** this is where Opus 4.7 earns its keep. The founder feeds Opus:

- The full Chilly Moose product guide PDF
- Three competitor articles on cooler performance
- Two academic papers on ice retention
- Five customer reviews of the 25L Ice Box from canoe trippers

A 1,400-word draft comes back in one pass. The founder rewrites the lede in his voice (an LLM cannot do that for you), keeps 80% of the body, ships it. **Two hours instead of two days.**

> **Non-obvious move:** the founder's voice is the moat. Use Claude as research and structure; keep the lede, the kicker, and any sentence with a strong opinion as fully human. That is the line that lets AI-assisted long-form rank in 2026's helpful-content world.

### Thursday — Shopify Liquid, theme, and dev work

The Wheeled Explorer PDP isn't rendering the size selector correctly on iOS Safari. The retainer dev is offline.

The founder opens Claude Code (or Cursor), points it at the theme repo via the Shopify AI Toolkit, and asks: "Inspect `sections/main-product.liquid` and `assets/product-form.js`. The variant picker is collapsing to 'Sold Out' on iOS Safari for the Wheeled Explorer (product handle: `35l-wheeled-explorer`). Fix it without breaking the desktop layout."

Sonnet 4.6 reads the section, identifies the bug (a JS event listener that doesn't fire on iOS), proposes the fix, applies it to a draft theme via the toolkit, and shows the diff. The founder previews on a real device, approves, publishes. **45 minutes, no retainer call.**

This is the workflow the Shopify AI Toolkit was built for. Sixteen skill files. Bulk operations included. Theme write access guarded behind explicit permission. ([fudge.ai walkthrough](https://www.fudge.ai/guides/shopify-ai-toolkit-claude-code-setup/))

### Friday — Reporting and decisions

Weekly numbers. The founder wants to know:

1. What did we sell, by category, vs last week and vs same week last year?
2. Which paid channel is paying back?
3. What are customers saying about the Brent Insulator after the spring launch?

He drops three exports into Opus 4.7 (long context wins here):

- Shopify orders export, last 60 days
- Triple Whale or Northbeam attribution export
- All Brent Insulator reviews from Okendo, last 8 weeks

In one prompt: "Summarize last week's revenue by category vs. prior week and YoY. Identify the channel with the lowest blended ROAS that's still scaling. Surface the three most-mentioned themes in the Brent reviews — both positive and negative."

Output: a 600-word brief. Two charts described in markdown for him to recreate. Three quotes pulled directly from reviews. **Half-day report → 25 minutes.**

The strategic value isn't the time saved. It is that Friday's questions used to be hard enough that they got skipped on busy weeks. Now they don't.

## The five Claude habits that compound

After dozens of Shopify deployments at X9Elysium, the operators who get the most leverage all do the same five things:

1. **Build projects, not prompts.** Claude Projects keep your brand voice, reference docs, and rules warm. A new teammate is productive the first hour.
2. **Pick one model as default and stop deciding.** Sonnet 4.6 for daily work. Open Opus 4.7 only when the task requires it.
3. **Write a one-page AI policy.** Who can use what. What data never leaves. Where Toolkit tokens live. Read once at onboarding. Re-read every six months.
4. **Run write operations through draft state for the first 30 days.** Theme drafts. Product drafts. Order drafts. Confidence comes from observing, not from trusting.
5. **Keep the human voice on the things that need it.** Founder POVs. Hero PDPs. The two emails a quarter that anchor brand. Everything else is fair game.

## What you should not use Claude for

- **Compliance copy.** Health claims, food allergen statements, regulatory disclosures. Use templates, not generation.
- **Inventory forecasting on a thin dataset.** If you have 18 months of data, fine. If you have 4 months, you need a human merchandiser, not a model.
- **Final brand positioning.** Use Claude as a sparring partner. Don't ship its first answer.
- **Anything legal-binding.** Privacy policy, terms, MSA. Use Claude as a researcher; have a lawyer redline.

## A 30-day rollout plan you can start Monday

**Week 1 — Foundations.**
- One Claude workspace plan. Three seats minimum.
- Brand-voice prompt, version 1.
- Three reference docs in a Project: shipping policy, returns policy, product guide.

**Week 2 — Catalog.**
- Pilot 50 SKUs through the new PDP workflow.
- Compare conversion rate vs control after 7 days.
- Iterate the brief.

**Week 3 — Support and email.**
- Wire Sonnet 4.6 into your support drafts (Gorgias / Re:amaze / manual).
- Move two Klaviyo flows to AI-assisted authoring.

**Week 4 — Toolkit.**
- Install the Shopify AI Toolkit on a staging theme.
- Run one bulk product update and one theme tweak through it.
- Document the operator workflow.

By day 30 you will have moved roughly 40% of catalog, support, and dev coordination work into a system that runs faster, costs less, and frees the operator to actually grow the business.
