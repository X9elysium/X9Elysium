Walk into any Shopify Plus store admin and look at the installed apps list. The pattern repeats across 60+ stores we've audited: 30 to 45 apps installed, 12–18 actively used, the rest are ghosts from old experiments, deprecated tools, or trial installs the merchant forgot to cancel. Each one is a quiet tax on speed, reliability, and conversion. This post is the founder framework for cleaning the stack without breaking what's working.

## The compounding cost of a single app

When you install a Shopify app, you're typically buying:

- A monthly subscription fee.
- Some functionality (reviews, upsells, popups, analytics, etc.).

What you're also buying — invisibly — is:

- One or more script tags injected into your theme, executing on every page load.
- A network request to the app's servers (and possibly multiple, depending on the app).
- A potential dependency on third-party cookies, which don't survive Safari/Firefox.
- A dependency on the app's vendor staying in business, supporting the app, and not breaking under your traffic.
- A code path that needs testing every time you make theme changes.
- A potential failure mode during BFCM peak.

The monthly fee is the visible cost. Everything else is the hidden tax.

## What "active" actually means

The metric most merchants use is "Have I logged into this app in the last 30 days?" That misses the point. The right question is: **does this app contribute measurable revenue or operational savings I can defend?**

Categories of apps that fail this test:

- **Trial installs that became permanent.** Tested 18 months ago, never canceled, still injecting scripts.
- **Solved-problem apps.** Solved a specific problem (e.g., a custom checkout field) that has since been solved natively in Shopify or by extensibility.
- **Decoration apps.** Countdown timers, social proof popups, "X people are viewing this product" widgets. Usually performance-negative and conversion-neutral or negative.
- **Duplicative apps.** Two reviews apps, two SMS platforms, two wishlist tools. Pick one.
- **Stale analytics.** That GA4 add-on you installed when GA4 launched, still running alongside your real GA4 setup.

A typical store has 6–12 apps in these categories.

## The audit framework

For every installed app, answer:

1. **Does it generate or save revenue?** Measurable amount, or a clear yes/no rationale.
2. **What is its performance cost?** Use Lighthouse, look at the network tab — does it inject blocking scripts?
3. **Is it duplicated?** Another app or native Shopify feature doing the same job?
4. **When was it last actively used?** Login data, dashboard activity.
5. **Is there a lighter alternative?** Many app categories have evolved — newer apps in the same space are often dramatically lighter.

Score each app red/yellow/green. Red apps go into the kill queue. Yellow apps go into the alternative-evaluation queue. Green apps stay.

For a typical $5M Plus store, the audit usually identifies:

- 5–10 apps to remove outright (red).
- 4–8 apps to evaluate alternatives for (yellow).
- 12–20 apps to keep (green).

## The order of operations for cleanup

**Step 1: Document.**
Before touching anything, list every installed app with: name, monthly cost, owner on the team, primary purpose, dependencies (does anything else rely on this app?).

**Step 2: Separate "set-and-forget" from "actively-managed."**
Some apps require attention to deliver value (Klaviyo, Gorgias). Others run silently (shipping calculators, currency converters). Different evaluation criteria.

**Step 3: Identify the kill list.**
Apps with no defensible revenue contribution. Decoration apps with negative performance impact. Duplicates. Stale analytics.

**Step 4: Test in a duplicate theme.**
Duplicate your live theme. Uninstall the kill-list apps in the duplicate. Run Lighthouse before/after on key page types (homepage, PDP, collection). Run a smoke test through checkout. Confirm nothing critical broke.

**Step 5: Theme-level cleanup.**
Many apps don't fully clean up at uninstall. Search your theme files for script references to the uninstalled apps. Remove. Use Shopify's theme inspector to verify no console errors after cleanup.

**Step 6: Push to live and monitor.**
Push the cleaned theme. Watch error logs and conversion metrics for 7 days. Roll back if anything degrades.

**Step 7: Repeat for the yellow list.**
For apps where you're swapping to a lighter alternative, do the same process: install the new one, configure, test, swap, clean up the old one.

## Specific app categories to audit

**Reviews apps.** Modern reviews apps (Junip, Okendo, Stamped) are dramatically lighter than legacy reviews tools. If you're on an older provider, the swap usually pays back in performance alone.

**Live chat / support apps.** Heavyweight live chat widgets (Tawk, older Tidio) can add 200–500KB to every page load. Newer offerings (Gorgias chat, Re:amaze, Crisp) are lighter. If you don't actually staff live chat outside business hours, consider replacing with an offline contact form during off-hours.

**Subscription apps.** Recharge, Skio, Loop Subscriptions, Smartrr — material differences in performance. If you've been on the same subscription app for 3+ years, the alternatives are worth comparing.

**Page builders.** PageFly, Shogun, GemPages — useful, but heavy. If you only use a page builder for 2–3 pages, consider rebuilding those as native Shopify sections and uninstalling the builder.

**Currency / language apps.** Most replaced by Shopify Markets. If you installed a currency switcher in 2020 and haven't migrated to Markets, that's an easy uninstall + native re-implementation.

**Popup / opt-in apps.** Klaviyo includes solid popup functionality. If you're paying for a separate Privy or Justuno on top, evaluate whether Klaviyo's native version is sufficient.

**Loyalty apps.** Smile, LoyaltyLion, Yotpo Loyalty — heavy by category. If your loyalty program has under 5% redemption activity, the cost in performance and subscription fees may exceed the value.

## Native-Shopify replacements you may have missed

Many apps installed in 2018–2022 have native Shopify equivalents now:

- **Currency conversion** → Shopify Markets.
- **Multi-language storefronts** → Shopify Markets + Translate & Adapt.
- **Custom product fields** → Metafields and metaobjects.
- **Bundle products** → Shopify Functions + Bundles app (free, native).
- **Simple discounts** → Native automatic discounts and Shopify Functions.
- **Shipping rate logic** → Shopify Functions for delivery customization.
- **Customer accounts** → New customer accounts (passwordless) shipped 2023.
- **Cart drawer** → Most modern themes ship with native cart drawers.

Migrating from app to native is rarely a single afternoon — you usually have data to move and configurations to re-do — but the long-term performance and reliability win is real.

## Performance numbers from real audits

A representative app audit we ran on a $7M ARR Plus store:

- **Apps before:** 38 installed, 22 actively scripted into the theme.
- **Apps after:** 18 installed, 14 actively scripted.
- **Mobile LCP:** 4.2s → 2.6s.
- **JavaScript bundle:** 1.4MB → 850KB.
- **App subscription costs:** $1,440/month → $890/month (saved $550/month).
- **Mobile conversion rate:** +9% over the following 60 days.
- **Annualized impact:** ~$420K incremental revenue + $6.6K saved subscription cost.

These numbers are typical, not exceptional.
